Bertrand's box paradox

There are three boxes,

  • box A contains two Gold Coins
  • box B contains a Gold Coin and a Silver Coin
  • box C contains two Silver Coins
The boxes are not labeled. Choose one box at random and pick one coin out from that box.
If that coin is Gold, what is the probability of finding another Gold coin on your second pick from that box?

It may seem intuitive that the probability would be 1/2, but the probability is actually 2/3.
Bertrand showed that if 1/2 were correct, it would result in a contradiction.
